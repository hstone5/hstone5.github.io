About Me

I am a PhD student in Psychology and Brain Sciences at UC Santa Barbara. My research interests include visual neuroscience, cortical visual prostheses, TMS-evoked phosphene perception, and computational models of visual prostheses.

Research

I am interested in how visual percepts can be generated, measured, and modeled through neural stimulation. My current work focuses on phosphene perception, TMS, EEG, and computational models of early visual cortex.

Research interests

  • Visual neuroscience
  • Cortical visual prostheses
  • TMS-evoked phosphenes
  • Artificial vision and brain-computer interfaces
