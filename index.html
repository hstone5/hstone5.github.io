Home

I am a PhD student in Psychology and Brain Sciences at UC Santa Barbara. My research interests include visual neuroscience, artificial vision, cortical visual prostheses, brain stimulation, and computational models of visual perception.

Research

I am interested in how visual percepts can be generated, measured, and modeled through neural stimulation. My current work focuses on phosphene perception, TMS, EEG, and computational models of early visual cortex.

Research interests

  • Visual neuroscience
  • Cortical visual prostheses
  • TMS-evoked phosphenes
  • Artificial vision and brain-computer interfaces
  • Computational models of V1
